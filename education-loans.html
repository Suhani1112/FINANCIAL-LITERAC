Education Loans in India

Recent Trends & Figures

  • Education loan sector has seen strong growth; total student loan debt exceeded ₹1.17 lakh crore by Jan 2024, 15% annual growth[9].
  • Leading states for education loan disbursal (FY 2022-25): Maharashtra (₹11,426 crore), Kerala (₹8,937 crore), Andhra Pradesh (₹8,101 crore)[8].
  • State Bank of India tops in education loan lending (₹32,311 crore disbursed, 2022–25)[8].
  • Public sector banks, NBFCs, and startups like GyanDhan, Propelld, MoneyView, PaySense, InCred are prominent lenders[10].

Eligibility & Process

  • Loans offered for UG, PG, Professional courses in India and abroad.
  • Typically covers tuition fee, living expenses, travel (for study abroad).
  • Applicant and parent/guardian as co-applicant; collateral/security may be required for large loans.
  • Interest rates 8–13% generally; moratorium period usually up to the course duration plus 6–12 months.

Repayment & Caution

  • Default rate on student loans is about 7–8%[9].
  • Net Performing Asset (NPA) levels challenging for some banks—but improving repayment discipline seen in last 2 years[8].
