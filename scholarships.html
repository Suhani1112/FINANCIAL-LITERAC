Scholarship Opportunities in India

Featured Programs

  • Reliance Foundation Undergraduate Scholarships (up to ₹2 lakh over degree, merit-cum-means, for UG students, 2025 applications closed)[5].
  • Swami Dayanand Scholarships (for B.Tech/MBBS first-year students, minimum 80% in Class 12, family income below ₹12 lakh, preference to government school students; 30% reserved for girls, deadline 30 September 2025)[5].
  • Many more national and state-level opportunities on platforms like Buddy4Study, including science, medical, engineering, SC/ST, girl child and social leadership scholarships[1].

Eligibility Highlights

Most scholarships require:

  • Indian citizenship and residency
  • High academic achievement (typically above 75–80%)
  • Need-based or means-based criteria
  • Specific exam qualifications (JEE/NEET/State boards/other)
  • Some programs reserve seats for women and underprivileged groups.

Application Tips

  • Prepare all documents (ID proofs, mark sheets, admission letters, income certificates) in advance.
  • Check each program’s criteria and deadlines carefully.
  • Many scholarships require direct application via their official websites. Beware of scams.
