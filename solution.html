Plan Your Budget with the 50-30-20 Rule

Simple guidance for smarter money management

What is the 50-30-20 Rule?

The 50-30-20 rule suggests dividing your income into three categories:

  • 50% Needs: rent, food, transport, healthcare.
  • 30% Wants: dining out, entertainment, hobbies.
  • 20% Savings/Investments: emergency fund, retirement, debt repayment, wealth building.

Step 4: Adjust Gradually

If 20% savings seem difficult, start smaller and increase gradually.

Investment & Savings Tips

For Students

  • Focus on building an emergency fund before investing heavily.
  • Start with low-risk investments such as recurring deposits or index funds (if affordable).
  • Try budgeting apps to track student expenses like tuition, rent, and books.
  • Avoid accumulating high-interest debt like credit cards.

For Working Professionals & Others

  • Contribute to retirement accounts or pension funds first.
  • Diversify with mutual funds, ETFs, or stocks.
  • Build an emergency fund covering at least 6 months of expenses.
  • Consider insurance (health/life) as part of financial planning.
  • Reinvest bonuses or salary hikes into savings/investments instead of lifestyle inflation.
